The Tax Foundation article identifies its underlying sources as state statutes, forms, and instructions. The app intentionally labels every state result an approximation because the table-driven v1 excludes state credits, phase-outs, recapture formulas, special deductions, local/city taxes, and filing-specific rules not expressible by the published schedule. Dollar “credits” in the source are not treated as deductions; dollar personal exemptions are. HOH uses the published Single schedule because the consolidated source supplies only Single and MFJ columns. These choices are visible estimate limitations, not hidden precision.

## Benefit tax-treatment sources

IRS Publication 15-B and the IRS cafeteria-plan FAQ support treating qualifying HSA/FSA/Section-125 payroll reductions as excluded from federal income-tax and FICA wages. HSA treatment assumes the selected owner is eligible under a qualifying HDHP for the modeled period. Because a general-purpose Health FSA usually disqualifies HSA contributions, entering both conservatively removes the HSA federal, FICA, and state exclusions while retaining every entered amount and showing a warning; a limited-purpose or post-deductible FSA is not currently a separate input. Tax exclusions stop at the modeled participant or household limit while the full entered what-if amount remains in the payroll ledger. When employee and employer HSA entries share a limit, the engine allocates the eligible exclusion to employee entries first and then employer entries; any remaining employer amount is treated as excess and added back to applicable taxable income and wages. This deterministic allocation is also disclosed in the visible tax notices. Selected age-55 HSA catch-ups are modeled per eligible owner. Warnings disclose that partial-year HSA eligibility, corrective distributions, plan remedies, excess-contribution or excise taxes, and eligible retirement-plan catch-ups are not modeled. Traditional 401(k) employee deferrals reduce federal taxable income but remain Social Security/Medicare wages; Roth 401(k) and ESPP payroll deductions are post-tax. Employer match and eligible employer HSA amounts do not reduce employee take-home. The default mapping is centralized in `src/domain/benefits.ts`; versioned state data overrides nonconforming CA/NJ HSA and PA 401(k), dependent-care, transit, and parking treatment, and custom entries expose all tax-treatment flags as an escape hatch.

The UI separates primary wages/bonus, spouse wages, and other ordinary non-wage income. Social Security caps independently per spouse; percentage benefits use the payroll owner selected on each benefit. “Other ordinary income” deliberately excludes payroll/self-employment tax and preferential capital-gains treatment, which the visible federal approximation notice states. Dependent-care FSA modeling warns when the entered amount exceeds modeled spouse wages because student/incapacitated-spouse exceptions are not inputs. Reimbursement-account and ESPP notices identify the remaining ledger/lookback assumptions without silently changing entered what-if amounts.

## Product landscape check

The feature list was rechecked on 2026-07-12 and remains a positioning input, not a parity contract:

- [YNAB targets](https://support.ynab.com/en_us/how-to-use-targets-rk5kkI9ks) confirms editable category targets, monthly/yearly/custom cadence, and rollover behavior. Kyle Financial adopts customizable categories and cadence, but remains an annual planning calculator rather than a transaction envelope system.
- [Monarch Reports](https://help.monarch.com/hc/en-us/articles/21846787088916-Using-Reports) confirms a Sankey cash-flow view plus category/group trend charts. Kyle Financial adopts a simpler income-allocation flow built for an iPhone plan view.
- [PocketGuard Leftover](https://pocketguard.com/help/leftover/) confirms the “Income − Expenses − Goals” leftover headline and explicitly permits a negative result. Kyle Financial’s “What’s left each month” is similarly immediate, but derives income from tax/benefit modeling rather than linked transactions.
- [EveryDollar’s product distinction](https://everydollar.help.ramseysolutions.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038295611-How-is-EveryDollar-different-from-other-budget-tools-) continues to offer a free manually maintained budget alongside an optional connected-account tier, supporting the viability of manual planning.
- [Copilot dashboard guidance](https://help.copilot.money/en/articles/6045480-dashboard-tab-overview) confirms an iOS-first, glanceable home view centered on spending progress and remaining budget. Kyle Financial adopts glanceability, not transaction tracking.
